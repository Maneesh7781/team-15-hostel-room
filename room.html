Room Allocation

Assign rooms based on availability and preferences.

  1. Residents must carry their ID cards at all times.
  2. Entry is restricted after 10:00 PM unless prior permission is granted.
  3. Noise levels must be kept low, especially during study hours.
  4. Visitors are allowed only during specified hours and must be registered at the front desk.
  5. Any damage to property will be charged to the resident responsible.

Facilities

  • Fully furnished rooms (single, double, or triple occupancy)
  • 24/7 security and CCTV surveillance
  • Wi-Fi enabled study rooms and common areas
  • Mess with nutritious and hygienic food
  • Laundry and housekeeping services
  • Recreational area with indoor games and TV

Types of Hostel Rooms

Single Room: One student per room. More privacy; often given to final-year or postgraduate students. Double Sharing: Two students per room. Most common type in many colleges. Triple/Multiple Sharing: Three or more students share a room. Usually for first-years or where space is limited. Dormitory-Style: Large room with multiple beds (common in some older hostels or for temporary housing).
